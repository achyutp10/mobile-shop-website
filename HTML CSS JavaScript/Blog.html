Internet & Technology

The Internet is a system architecture that allows various computer networks all over the world to interact with one another, changing communications and business. The Internet, sometimes described as a "network of networks," originally arose in the United States in the 1970s but did not gain widespread popularity until the early 1990s. By 2020, it is predicted that 4.5 billion people, or more than half of the world's population, would have access to the Internet....

Impacts

The Internet is so vast and powerful that it may be used for almost any information-based purpose, and it is available to anybody who connects to one of its constituent networks. It promotes human interaction through social media and allows individuals to cooperate from different areas. The Internet has given birth to a plethora of "e-businesses" that perform the majority of their sales and services online. According to estimates, 95 % of all available information has been digitized and made available over the internet. Because of the internet, communication, knowledge availability, and social interaction have all altered tremendously. However, like with all major technical advances, the internet has both positive and negative consequences on society....
